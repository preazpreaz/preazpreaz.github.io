Alejandro's Top Five Favorite Albums

5. Some Rap Songs by Earl Sweatshirt

After a long awaited 3 years since his last project, Earl Sweatshirt's 2018 magnum opus Some Rap Songs is a true display of lyricism and story telling. In this album, Earl dives into vunerable themes such as his struggle with mental health following several losses in his life, including his father and uncle who had helped raised him. The production on the album is equally nothing short but phenomenal, as Earl combines a unique production style of sample flips from old records, remixing these elder songs into something that sounds fresh and new. Earl is unafraid to put on a display of vunerability in exchange for some of the most emotionally touching lines that reflect on Earl's state of mind at the time of recording the album. Don't let the bare simplicity of the cover and album title fool you, as paired with avant-garde jazz, this album is a perfect blend that makes the border between dreams and Hip-Hop dizzy.

4. In The Court Of The Crimson King by King Crimson

An album that is sonically three decades ahead of its time, In The Court Of The Crimson King, released in 1969, is undoubtedly one of the most influential musical projects of all time. Combining different sonic elements of rock and jazz, the album would set the stage for the rise of progressive rock in the late 1960's. Apart from being a sonic masterpiece split across a series of just five tracks, the album's lyrics divulge into relevant topics of its time period. The album is a critique of the Vietnam War, with in-depth lines such as "The yellow jester does not play, but gently pulls the strings/ And smiles as the puppets dance in the court of the crimson king" symbolizing the consequences of war and corrupt politicians.

3. Yeezus by Kanye West

Released in 2013, Kanye West's Yeezus has proven to be one of the most nonconformist and rebellious albums of all time, displaying West's true identity as a contrarian. The albums heavy industrial and experimental sound is a complete 180 from West's previous album My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy, showcasing West trading in his soothing soul and accessible production made for West to escape the turmoil of controversy from past events as well as tragedies for the albums unorthodox and raw sound combined with West's egotistical nature at full display (hence the title, Yeezus). This radical sound helps assist the theme of Kanye West being nonconforming; Kanye expresses his thirst for success in the fashion industry despite criticsm (which would eventually evolve to West's $8 billion dollar fashion line with Adidas) to his struggles in the music industry. The beauty of Yeezus is despite it's self-centered and aggressive nature, West sheds his mask to display vunerability in tracks like "Guilt Trip" and "Blood on the Leaves". In the mid-way point of the opening track, "On Sight", West includes a soul sample that states "He'll give us what we need/ it may not be what we want", being prophetic to how West's album would become a cultural reset in the genre of Hip-Hop. The albums influence, although it being a fairly young album, has already seen shape into the works of different artists like Travis Scott's 2023 UTOPIA, who have adopted the raw sound and nature of Yeezus.
